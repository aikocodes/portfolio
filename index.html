üçì Who I Am üçì

I'm a designer and developer passionate about building human-centered systems at the intersection of technology and culture.

Born in Kyoto and raised across New Jersey and Tokyo, I've grown up navigating different cultures, languages, and ways of thinking. I am now based in Claremont, California, where I study at Pomona College as a Yanai Tadashi (UNIQLO) Scholar.

My academic interests center on examining the societal impact of advances in AI through computational approaches, including data analysis and data visualization from a computer science perspective, as well as theoretical inquiry from a digital media studies framework. In addition, I am passionate about textile and costume design and am particularly drawn to research at the intersection of computation and art.

I have experience across e-commerce design and data analytics, web and app development, UI/UX design, and strategy consulting. My work focuses on translating complex data and systems into intuitive, user-centered experiences, bridging technical rigor with thoughtful design and strategic insight.
